## 1. Why the Stocks Shot Up 10%+ Today

The primary engine behind today's rally was a pair of stunning fiscal earnings reports from Dell and NetApp that proved enterprise AI demand is accelerating at an eye-popping scale.

### **Dell Technologies (DELL) — Up ~33-35%**

Dell delivered what analysts are calling an "absolute earnings sensation" for its fiscal Q1.

* **The Numbers:** Total revenue jumped 88% year-over-year to $43.8 billion (smashing estimates of ~$35.8B). Adjusted EPS came in at $4.86 vs. the $2.96 expected.
* **AI Server Rocket:** Dell’s AI server revenue surged **757% year-over-year** to $16.1 billion, with fresh AI orders hitting $24.4 billion.
* **The Guidance:** Management fundamentally re-rated the stock by raising full-year revenue guidance to an unprecedented $165–$169 billion range (Wall Street was expecting $142.5B).

### **NetApp (NTAP) — Up ~15-19%**

Data storage has long been flagged as the next critical bottleneck for AI, and NetApp's fiscal Q4 report proved it.

* **The Numbers:** Revenue grew 12.5% year-over-year to $1.95 billion, beating consensus. All-flash storage arrays (crucial for training data ingestion pipelines) hit record revenues of $1.22 billion (up 18%).
* **AI Traction:** NetApp logged more than 1,100 AI and data-preparation wins in fiscal 2026.
* **Capital Return:** They coupled a massive FY2027 guidance beat (predicting $8.70 to $9.00 EPS vs. lower consensus) with a fresh **$1 billion share buyback authorization**.

### **HP Inc. (HPQ) & Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE) — Up 7% to 18%**

HP and HPE rose sharply in sympathy. Dell's monster quarter acted as a rising tide for all enterprise hardware boats. It proved to institutional investors that enterprise enterprise clients are aggressively spending capital to stand up "AI Factories," driving massive secular demand for liquid-cooled server racks, high-throughput storage systems, and AI-enabled edge PCs (benefiting HPQ).
